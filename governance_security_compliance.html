1. Digital Governance Structure

Steering Committee (8–10 members)

Chair: Her Excellency, the Executive President of OMSB

Purpose: Set strategic direction, ratify priorities and policies, approve funding, and track KPIs and risk. Reports to the OMSB Board.

Indicative Membership:

· Director of Education & Training

· Director of Examinations & Assessment

· Director of Accreditation & Quality

· Director of Information Technology

· Director of Finance & Administration

· Director of Human Resources & Org Dev

· Director of Legal/Compliance

· Director of Communications & PR

· (Ex-officio): CISO & CDO

Cadence: Monthly governance sessions, quarterly Board status, KPI dashboard, and risk register sign-off.

Program Leadership

Program Manager (Manager): Reports directly to the Steering Committee. Accountable for delivering the strategy, benefits realization, budget, schedule, risks, vendor management, and change enablement.

Assistant Manager: Manages day-to-day coordination, PMO routines, issue/action tracking, and dependency management.

Digital Transformation Team

Core delivery team under the Program Manager: Program Manager, Assistant Manager, Community Manager, and Initiatives & Projects Managers.

Community Manager — Scope:
  • Own OMSB digital strategy brand, narrative, and style guide.
  • Run stakeholder awareness and publish service updates.
  • Lead change communications, feedback loops, and report sentiment/uptake metrics.

Initiatives & Projects — Scope

  • Each initiative has a Project/Initiative Manager and a Business Owner.
  • PMO controls: scope baselines, RAID logs, earned-value/velocity tracking, benefits profiles with KPIs.
  • Ways of Working: Weekly DT stand-up; bi-weekly reviews; monthly Steering Committee.

4) Supporting Governance Boards (Decision Forums)

IT Governance & Architecture Board

Chair: Director of IT (with Enterprise Architect)

Mandate: Architecture standards, integration patterns, tech selections, change requests, exceptions.

Data Governance Committee

Chair: CDO (or data steward)

Mandate: Data standards, quality, access/classification, open-data, privacy impact assessments.

Information Security Oversight (CISO)

Lead: CISO

Mandate: ISMS/ISO 27001 controls, risk assessments, incident approvals, MFA/privileged access, security by design.

5) Decision Rights & RACI (Summary)

Steering Committee: A/R for strategy, funding, priorities, major policies, risk appetite.

Business Owners: A for business outcomes, process adoption, operational handover.

Program Manager: A/R for delivery, budgets, dependencies, benefits tracking.

IT Gov & Arch Board: A/R for architecture standards, integrations, exceptions.

Assistant Manager / PMO: R for plans, reports, RAID, change logs, cadence.

Data Gov Committee: A/R for data standards, quality, lawful use.

Initiative/Project Managers: R for scope/schedule/cost/quality.

CISO Function: A/R for security controls and incidents.

Community Manager: R for comms, branding, awareness, feedback.

Foundational Pillars

Policy Framework & Compliance

Goal: Near 100% compliance with Government's ICT controls by 2027.

  • National Policies (By 2026): Adopt outstanding national IT policies, including the National Digital Accessibility Policy and an Internet Usage Policy.
  • Standards Alignment: Align with ISO 27001 and Oman’s national cybersecurity framework.
  • Required Documentation: Publish an Information Security Governance Framework and an ICT BC/DR Plan by 2026.
  • Verification: Conduct annual IT audits to verify compliance and identify gaps.

Cybersecurity Enhancements

Goal: Achieve ISO 27001 certification by 2028.

  • ISMS: Implement ISO/IEC 27001-aligned ISMS.
  • Access Control: Role-based access, least privilege, MFA.
  • Data Protection: Encrypt at rest & in transit (TLS).
  • Monitoring & Testing: Annual VA/PT; establish 24/7 SOC by 2028.
  • Incident Readiness: IR team & drills.
  • Privacy: PIAs for new services; comply with evolving data protection regs.

Risk Management

Integrate digital transformation risks into ERM.

  • Integration: Key risks (delivery, change, vendor, cyber) tracked in ERM.
  • Risk Register: Likelihood/impact, owners; reviewed every Steering Committee.
  • Mitigation Examples:
    • Change: Communications, training, end-user involvement.
    • Vendors: SLAs, code escrow/KT, backups.
    • Cyber: Monitoring, firewalls, optional cyber insurance.
  • Contingency: Triggered plans when risks materialize.

Operating Model & Reporting Lines

OMSB Board

↳ Steering Committee (Her Excellency, Executive President — Chair)

↳ Program Manager (reports directly to Steering Committee)

↳ Assistant Manager

↳ Digital Transformation Team (Community Manager, Initiatives & Projects Managers)

↳ Advisory/Approvals:

  • IT Governance & Architecture Board
  • Data Governance Committee
  • CISO / Security Oversight

Key Ways of Working & Transparency:

Cadence: Weekly DT stand-up; bi-weekly reviews; monthly Steering Committee; quarterly Board review.

Controls: PMO toolkit, privacy/security by design, architecture checks.

Transparency: Public roadmap highlights, service dashboards, change calendars.

Benefits: KPIs (SLAs, satisfaction, adoption) rolled up to a benefits dashboard.

Strong governance, rigorous security, and strict compliance are foundational to OMSB’s digital strategy. By setting up the right committees and policies, OMSB enables controlled, transparent execution. Hardening security and managing risks protects stakeholders and builds confidence—creating a culture of accountability essential for sustainable transformation.
