Governance, Security, and Compliance

Robust governance and strong security are the cornerstones of this digital strategy, ensuring that initiatives are well-managed and risks are controlled. OMSB will institute multiple layers of oversight and align all activities with best practices and national regulations.

Digital Governance Structure

OMSB will formalize a governance framework to oversee the digital transformation. This involves establishing dedicated bodies and clarifying roles to ensure clear strategic direction and accountability.

Digital Transformation Steering Committee (DTSC)

Chaired by a senior executive, this high-level committee provides **strategic direction**, prioritizes projects, approves key policies, and monitors progress/KPIs, reporting directly to the OMSB Board.

IT Governance & Architecture Board

Focuses on **technical decisions**, including standards for systems, integration, architecture approvals, and managing exception requests.

Data Governance Committee

Responsible for **data standardization, data quality issues**, and ensuring data usage complies with privacy rules, with cross-department representation.

Key Roles: An Information Security Officer (or CISO) and a Chief Data Officer will be empowered to enforce policies. Clear **RACI matrices** will be defined for all major initiatives.

Policy Framework and Compliance

OMSB will proactively update its internal policy framework to achieve **near 100% compliance** with the Governmentâ€™s ICT controls by 2027 and align with international standards like ISO 27001.

  • Adoption of National Policies (By 2026): Formal adoption of the **National Digital Accessibility Policy** and an **Internet Usage Policy** for employees.
  • Security Alignment: Existing policies will be reviewed and strengthened in line with **ISO 27001** and Omanâ€™s national cybersecurity framework.
  • Mandatory Documentation (By 2026): Documenting an **Information Security Governance Framework** and an **ICT Business Continuity/Disaster Recovery Plan**.
  • Verification: Conducting **annual IT audits** (internal or external) to verify compliance and identify gaps for remediation.

Achieving this level of policy alignment and proven compliance allocates a significant weight (40%) to institutional readiness under the Government Digital Transformation Excellence criteria.

Cybersecurity Enhancements

Protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of OMSBâ€™s systems and data is paramount. The goal is to implement an **Information Security Management System (ISMS)** aligned with ISO/IEC 27001 standards by the late 2020s.

Technical Security Controls

  • **Access Controls:** Enforcing Role-Based Access (RBAC), Least Privilege principle, and **MFA** for administrators and sensitive system users.
  • **Encryption:** Encrypting data **at rest** (databases, storage) and **in transit** (using TLS for all web/app traffic).
  • **Testing:** Conducting **Vulnerability Assessments and Penetration Testing** on critical applications at least annually.

Incident Response & Monitoring

  • **Incident Response:** Establishing a dedicated **Incident Response Team** and procedure, including periodic drills to test readiness.
  • **24/7 Monitoring:** Achieving a functional **Security Operations Center (SOC)** capability by 2028 (in-house or managed services).
  • **Target:** Achieving **ISO 27001 certification by 2028** as a strategic target.

Privacy protection is also key: new digital services will undergo **Privacy Impact Assessments (PIA)**, and ethical guidelines will accompany the introduction of emerging tech like **AI** to ensure fairness and auditability.

Risk Management

Effective governance includes proactive risk management. OMSB will integrate digital transformation risks into its **Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) framework** to ensure the transformation stays on course.

Key Elements of Risk Management

►

**Risk Register:** A comprehensive risk register will be maintained, logging risks with their **likelihood and impact**, and assigning owners for mitigation. The DTSC will review this register in each meeting.

►

**Contingency Planning:** The governance bodies will execute contingency plans if any risk materializes (e.g., project delays), such as bringing in additional resources or adjusting timelines.

►

**Mitigation Examples:** For **change resistance**, heavy investment in communications and training; for **vendor risks**, clear SLAs, code escrow, and knowledge transfer clauses in contracts.
