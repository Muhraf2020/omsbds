Executive Summary

Executive Summary

The Oman Medical Specialty Board (OMSB) stands at a critical juncture in its digital evolution. Over the past decade, OMSB has piloted important digital initiatives (e.g. the MASAR e-portfolio prototype, a new LMS, an online exam engine, and AR/VR simulation trials), but core processes and data remain fragmented across siloed systems. This Digital Transformation Strategy 2026–2035 presents a comprehensive roadmap to modernize OMSB’s operations, fully align with Oman’s national digital agenda, and position OMSB as a regional leader in digitally-enabled medical education and certification by 2036. The strategy directly supports Oman Vision 2040’s emphasis on excellent governance, human capital development, and world-class healthcare/education services underpinned by digital transformation. It translates OMSB’s 2040 strategic objectives and the national e-health vision into a phased and funded program of initiatives.

Strategic Ambition

By 2035, OMSB will operate as a seamless, data-driven, and user-centric ecosystem unifying residency and fellowship management, e-assessment, accreditation, credentialing, and CPD tracking under a single secure cloud platform. All stakeholders – trainees, faculty, administrators, healthcare partners, and regulators – will engage through intuitive digital channels that provide real-time information, personalized insights, and globally trusted credentials. This transformation will enable faster, paperless services, improved decision-making through data, and a superior user experience for all OMSB beneficiaries, ultimately elevating the quality and efficiency of postgraduate medical training in Oman.

Key Outcomes by 2035

The strategy defines clear targets to measure success. By fully executing the plan, OMSB aims to achieve the following major outcomes by 2035:

  • Process Excellence — Reduce certification and credentialing cycle times by 60%; achieve 95% on-time exam result publication; and fully automate CPD compliance tracking.
  • Interoperability — Implement API-driven integration across all OMSB systems and two-way data exchange with the Ministry of Health’s Nabd Al Shifa electronic health record (EHR), universities, and international accreditation bodies.
  • Advanced Analytics & AI — Deploy predictive workforce modeling, “at-risk trainee” early-alert systems, AI-driven recommendations for CPD, and process optimizations embedded in daily operations.
  • User Experience — Attain ≥90% user satisfaction among trainees, faculty, and partners by offering mobile-first services with real-time notifications, digital credential wallets, and bilingual (Arabic/English) support.
  • Governance & Security — Achieve ISO 27001 certification for information security and full compliance with national ICT policies (e.g. 100% WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility on all platforms), with robust role-based access controls and audit trails for every transaction.
  • Sustainability — Establish an “evergreen” IT environment with quarterly feature releases, ensure ≥50% spare system capacity for growth, and evolve the MASAR platform into a self-financing model (e.g. via a joint venture or service revenues) by 2036.

Investment & Phasing

Realizing these outcomes will require sustained investment and a staged approach. The transformation program is budgeted at approximately OMR 22.2 million over 10 years. It will be executed in three phases — Foundation (2026–2028) – focusing on core infrastructure, integration, and quick-win e-services; Optimization & Expansion (2029–2032) – deploying advanced analytics, AI, blockchain credentialing, and achieving full service digitization; and Continuous Innovation (2033–2036) – institutionalizing a culture of innovation, predictive planning, and global interoperability. Each phase has defined milestones and deliverables to ensure measurable progress (detailed in the roadmap section).

Strategic Pillars

To structure the transformation, six strategic pillars have been identified, each encompassing key initiatives and projects:

  1. Modernize & Automate Core Processes — End-to-end digitization and simplification of all OMSB services and workflows.
  2. Unify Data & Break Down Silos — Creation of a single source of truth via a cloud data lake, data warehouse, and Master Professional Index for all trainees/professionals.
  3. Embed Analytics & AI — Integration of real-time dashboards, predictive analytics, and AI tools to drive data-informed decisions and proactive support (e.g. predictive trainee performance alerts).
  4. Deliver User-Centric Experiences — Development of mobile-first, multi-channel digital services with a unified portal and single sign-on, providing a consistent and accessible user experience.
  5. Strengthen Governance & Security — Establishment of robust IT governance structures, cybersecurity frameworks, and compliance programs (aligning with ISO standards and national policies).
  6. Empower Workforce & Foster Innovation — Comprehensive change management, staff upskilling, and an innovation culture to ensure adoption of new systems and continuous improvement.

By executing this strategy, OMSB will not only fulfill its regulatory and educational mandate more efficiently, but also emerge as a regional exemplar in digitally integrated medical education and licensure. This will attract strategic partnerships, enable seamless mobility of Omani specialists (through trusted digital credentials), and contribute significantly to Oman’s health system performance and workforce readiness — all in line with Oman Vision 2040.
